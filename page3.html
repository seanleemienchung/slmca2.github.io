Shutter, Aperture, ISO

These three functions set the basis for photography.
Let's try to understand them!

Image Source: SCHOOL OF DESIGN




Shutter

It's the speed at which the shutter of the camera closes. A fast shutter speed creates a shorter exposure (the amount of light the camera takes in) and a slow shutter speed gives the photographer a longer exposure.

Learn more: CREATIVELIVE




Aperture

Aperture is the opening in the camera lens. A larger hole allows more light to hit the sensor, lightening your photos. A smaller hole allows less light to hit the sensor, darkening your photos.

Learn more: DIGITAL PHOTOGRAPHY SCHOOL




ISO

ISO is your camera's sensitivity to light as it pertains to either film or a digital sensor. A lower ISO value means less sensitivity to light, while a higher ISO means more sensitivity.

Learn more: PHOTOGRAPHYLIFE
